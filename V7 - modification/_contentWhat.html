    • Understand Stress

    Stress is:

    • a feeling of emotional / physical tension
    • body's reaction to a challenge / demand / threat
    • body's natural defense against predators and dangers
    • refer as the fight-or-flight mechanism

    Stressor is:

    • the factor that causes the state of strain / tension
    • body's reaction to a challenge / demand / threat

    Anxiety is:

    • body's natural response to stress.
    • a feeling of fear or apprehension about the upcoming event
    • feeling stress even though there is no stress-causing factor
    • extreme anxiety is when stress which continues after the stressor is gone
    1 / 7

    There are two types of stress:

    • Acute Stress
    • Chronic Stress
    Acute Stress is a short term stress which help us to manage dangerous situations. This is the more common form of stress. This type of stress is easy-come-easy-go. It also occurs when doing something new or exciting. Acute stressors are often new and tend to have a clear and immediate solution. Even with some difficult challenges, there are usually possible ways to solve the problem or get out of the situation.

    eg. Slam on the brakes, have fight with someone, ski down a steep slope etc.

    Chronic Stress is a long term stress which might leads to health problem. This is because our body gets used to staying in alert condition, which causes the body to be alerted even though there is no danger. It occurs when a person can find no way to avoid their stressors and stops seeking solutions. A traumatic experience in early life can also contribute to chronic stress. In long term, it might be harmful to our physical and/or mental health.

    eg. Financial struggle, unhappy marriage, trouble at work or other social life

    Where does it come from?


    Event or thought that makes you feel frustrated, angry, or nervous.

    How do our body react to stress?


    When facing danger or challenge, the body activates resources that help people to get to safety as fast as possible. The body produces larger quantities of the follwing hormones: cortisol, epinephrine, and norepinephrine. These trigger the following physical reactions:
    • release of hormones
    • brain alerting
    • muscles tensing
    • increasing pulse (protecting body itself)
    • faster breathing
    • slower digestive system
    • decrease in immune activity
    • decrease in sleepiness

    Signals of Stress


    Warning! Warning!
    Your body will give you some warning to let you know that you are struggling with too much stress.

    Here are some symptoms when your body can hardly or cannot handle the stress:
    • Difficult to concentrate / focus on tasks
    • Lower immune system
      get sick more often
    • Body aches and Headache
    • Depression or anxiety
    • Easy to get irritated, emotional (angry, anxious)
    • Trouble of sleeping

    Negative About Stress


    When body continue be staying in tensed situation for weeks or months, it is likely to cause some health issues.

    Here are some common symptoms of continuous stress:
    • High blood pressure
    • Heart disease
    • Obesity and Diabetes
    • Depression or anxiety
    • Skin problems, such as acne or eczema
    • Menstrual problems
    • Worsen health conditions in some cases
      eg. Diarrhea or constipation, Forgetfulness, Frequent aches, Headaches, Lack of focus, Tiredness, Trouble sleeping, Weight loss or gain
